There isn't really a lot of interest to say here. I’ve always loved technology. I was the kind of kid who had to read the instruction book before playing a new game, even though the controls were almost always jump, fire/attack and super attack. For those reading this that can’t quite imagine that, it was a very long time ago when a console controller only had a directional pad and two buttons. That wasn’t even the start of it, the first game I ever played came on a cassette tape and took about 7 minutes to load, and that was if it worked the first time. No disc, no internal hard drive, no operating system. Ever since then I have always been excited about what ever the next generation of tech would look like. Computers, consoles, cameras, etc. Yet somehow, I spent most of my working life in retail. It was never something I was passionate about, tech was what I was passionate about, retail was just something I was good at while tech was my hobby to play with. I wish I’d made the switch to a tech-based job sooner. Here's to moving to a job I can truly be happy in.
